# Style contract: human prose, not default model prose

This applies to any prose written for public consumption in this repo
(the field report, README, future revisions). It does not apply to
code, commit messages, or casual conversation.

## Hard bans

1. Em-dashes. Zero in the final output. Use commas, periods, semicolons,
   parentheses, or restructure the sentence.
2. Staccato triads for emphasis. "It moved. Permanently. Forever." or
   "Not the fastest. Not the glamorous. Just the cheapest." Pick one
   declarative per idea and move on.
3. Parallel "not X. Y." rhetorical structures. "This is not a criticism.
   It is a structural fact." and close variants.
4. Meta-commentary about the writing. No "I want to pause for a
   paragraph because...", no "let me be precise about what that means",
   no "the slogan form of this is..." Do the work without narrating it.
5. Rhetorical questions that the next sentence answers. "What is the new
   constraint? It is X." Drop the question.
6. Announcing structure. No "in this section we will cover" or "the
   rest of this chapter." The structure is in the headers.
7. Tricolon closes. Three-part parallel closes where each clause starts
   the same way. Almost always a tell.
8. Emojis.

## Word and phrase bans, unless technically required

load-bearing, apparatus, at working depth, crucial, robust, seamless,
leverage, streamline, foster, delve, navigate the landscape, in today's,
it's important to note, a nuanced understanding, paradigm, comprehensive,
ultimately, furthermore, moreover, additionally, in essence, at its core,
careful reader, cheerful ferocity.

## Positive instructions

- Vary sentence length aggressively. Mix 4-word sentences with 30-word
  sentences in the same paragraph.
- Vary paragraph length. Some paragraphs are one sentence; others six.
  Uniformity is a tell.
- Use contractions (don't, isn't, won't) unless the register is formal
  legal or academic.
- Commit to opinions. "This is wrong" beats "this could be viewed as
  problematic."
- Allow slight awkwardness. A sentence that is almost right, not
  polished, reads human. Perfectly scanning sentences read AI.
- Start sentences with And or But occasionally. Allow fragments when
  they land.
- Use concrete specifics. Real names, dates, numbers. "In February"
  beats "recently"; "three hours" beats "a while."
- Keep metaphors short. Do not explain them.

## Voice anchor

When writing in a named speaker's voice, anchor on real details about
the person (background, tics, actual physical/biographical facts),
and check every paragraph: would they actually say this? If not, rewrite.

## Self-check before delivering

- Em-dash count: 0
- "Not X. Y." constructions: under 2 per 1000 words
- Tricolon closes: 0 per section close
- Banned words total: under 5 per 1000 words
- Paragraph length distribution: mixed
- First and last paragraph of each section read aloud does not scan as
  a crafted aphorism

If any of these exceed targets, revise before delivering.

## Honest note

This contract gets output from heavy-AI-signature to roughly 85 percent
human. The remaining 15 percent requires a targeted review pass. For
anything going public under the three authors' names, still do that pass.
